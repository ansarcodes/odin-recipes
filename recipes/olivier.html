Description

Olivier salad is a traditional Russian potato salad made with finely diced potatoes, carrots, pickles, boiled eggs, sweet peas, onions, and bologna or ham, all tossed with a simple dressing of mayonnaise, salt, and pepper. Typically, you’ll find it served at family gatherings or on holidays. If you grew up in a Slavic home, you likely have memories of eating a big bowl of it on Christmas.

The original recipe for olivier potato salad was created by renowned Russian chef Lucien Olivier back in the 1860s. He was an extremely celebrated head chef at the Hermitage, one the most famous restaurants in Moscow’s history.

Ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Carrots
  • Eggs
  • Ham
  • Pickles
  • Canned sweet peas
  • Canned sweet corn
  • Mayonnaise

Steps

  1. Boil the carrots, potatoes and eggs(hard boil eggs) until they are easily pierced with a fork. Set the ingredients aside and let them cool down to room temperature.
  2. Dice the salad ingredients. Using sharp knife dice the boiled carrots, potatoes, eggs into small cubes. Add in a large bowl. Dice ham and pickles the same way and add into bowl.
  3. Open canned sweet peas and corn and remove the water/juice from the can. Add them into bowl too.
  4. Toss the salad ingredients with the mayonnaise and continue to mix until ingredients are well combined.
  5. Done. Now you can put the dish on a plate and serve it.
